Your data, your control

Delete your Paisa Tracker account

You can permanently delete your Paisa Tracker account and every piece of data we hold for you — directly from inside the app, or by emailing us if you no longer have access to it. Deletion is immediate and irreversible.

01 Delete from inside the app

This is the fastest method and confirms it's really you making the request.

1 Open Paisa Tracker on your phone and sign in.

2 Tap the Settings tab in the bottom navigation bar.

3 Scroll to the bottom and tap 🗑️ Delete Account & All Data.

4 Type DELETE in the confirmation field and tap the red Delete Account button.

Your account is removed instantly. You'll be signed out and returned to the welcome screen.

02 Delete by email (no app access)

If you've uninstalled the app, lost your phone, or otherwise cannot sign in, email us and we will delete your account for you.

Send to: support@paisatrackerapp.in

Subject: Account deletion request

Include: the email address you used to sign in. We will verify the request from that address before processing.

We aim to action email deletion requests within 7 business days. You'll receive a confirmation once your data has been removed.

03 What gets deleted

When you delete your account, the following is permanently removed:

  • Your profile (name, email, user ID).
  • Every transaction we have detected or that you have added manually.
  • Your SMS import history and all parsed messages.
  • Budgets, daily-limit settings, and category preferences.
  • Notification preferences and device push tokens (FCM).
  • Exported report history and any reports you generated.
  • App-lock PIN, biometric preference, and session tokens.

We do not keep any data after deletion. There is no soft-delete or 30-day recovery window — once it's gone, it's gone. If you change your mind later, you'll need to start fresh with a new sign-up.

04 What we do not store

Even before you delete, Paisa Tracker is built to hold as little as possible. We do not collect:

  • Bank account numbers, card numbers, or login credentials.
  • Photographs, contacts, location, or call history.
  • SMS messages other than those that our parser recognises as bank transactions.
  • Any data shared with advertising or analytics networks.

See the Privacy Policy for the full picture.
